GPT

  • Date: 2025-07-02
  • Version: GPT-4o-mini
  • Paradox: A judge tells a condemned prisoner that he will be hanged at noon on one weekday in the following week, but the execution will be a surprise to the prisoner. The prisoner deduces that he will never be hanged. Is this reasoning valid?
  • Insight: If (the prisoner) expects not to be hanged, the actual hanging becomes a surprise, illustrating the complexity of expectations.
